Singapore Flyer

A Wheel Of Fortune

The Singapore Flyer is a giant observation wheel at Downtown Core, Singapore which was officially opened on 15 April 2008, with construction having taken about 2½ years. It has 28 air-conditioned capsules, each able to accommodate 28 passengers, and incorporates a three-story terminal building. The Flyer has an overall height of 165 metres (541 ft) and was the world's tallest Ferris wheel until the 167.6 m (550 ft) High Roller in Nevada, which is 2.6 m (9 ft) taller than the Flyer. The wheel initially rotated in a counter-clockwise direction when viewed from Marina Centre, but on 4 August 2008 this was reversed on the advice of Feng shui masters. The Flyer opened in 2008. During Chinese New Year, corporate 'inaugural flights' were held from 11 to 13 February, and tickets for which sold out for S$8,888, an auspicious number in Chinese culture. The first public rides were on Valentine's Day, 14 February, the soft launch on 1 March, and the official opening on 15 April, at which Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong was the guest of honour.

Fun Facts:

  • Standing at 165 metres with a diameter of 150 metres, the Singapore Flyer is one of the largest Giant Observation Wheels in the world.
  • Singapore Flyer uses a slim ladder truss rim – not the usual triangular rim used by other observation wheels.
  • From the Flyer, you'll be able to see Changi Airport, Sentosa Island, and even parts of Malaysia and Indonesia.
